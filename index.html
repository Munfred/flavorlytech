Aroma Sensory Training

A kit with 10 aromas for sensory training based on patented technology to train olfaction for families of scents. Developed for sommelier students and enthusiasts, as well as people with anosmia (loss of sense of smell) due to neurological issues or COVID-19. The kit was created by brazilian scientists Dr. Amanda Reitenbach and Paula dos Santos

About Dr. Amanda Reitenbach

Dr. Reitenbach holds a PhD Neuroengineering. She studied food science and technology in her undergraduate and graduate studies. Most of her professional career has been devoted to beer science and related topics. She has trained thousands of students in Brazil and Latin America through her company Science of Beer. Her postdoctoral research was focused on multisensory studies for beverages and the neurogenetics of sensory perceptions, which formed the basis of the aroma sensory training kit.

Part of her PhD research was done in germany VLB Berlin, a research intstitute focused on the brewing industry, and the degree was awarded by the Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC). This research involved training and validating sensory panels focused on specific beer attributes, as well as developing an electronic nose to identify volatile compounds in beer. Amanda's master's thesis focused on the development of a probiotic beer, which offers numerous health benefits.

How does it work?

Usage is simple: Perform a few inhalations from the scents of a tube for 5 to 8 seconds.


Suggested use: Up to three times a day, with different aromas each time.

Sensory Education

Sensory training, in addition to functioning as a technique to expand the sensory library of professionals, can also be used as physiotherapy for people who have experienced or are experiencing anosmia (loss of smell).

Daily training has been shown to be effective for identifying aromas and recovering the sense of smell, and serves as a way to make references for the formation of a mental library of aromas.

Our training protocol was developed based on scientific research in the field of sensory analysis.

Our kits are divided into olfactory families:
Hops, wine, distilled drinks, coffee, tea and wood.
